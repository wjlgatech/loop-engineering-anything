Your AI writes a CLI, you eyeball it, you ship it. It's frozen at "good enough" the moment it's born.

Nothing closes the gap between "it exists" and "it's actually good."

We open-sourced **loop-engineering-anything** to close it — on your machine, your code never leaves.

Point it at any API or codebase. It builds an agent-native CLI, then loops:

→ an independent referee grades it (5 dimensions, A–F, against real captured payloads — never the model grading itself)
→ it refactors the weakest dimension
→ every accepted fix becomes a regression test, so a solved problem never comes back
→ it stops at target grade, plateau, budget, or a safety block — and rolls back anything that doesn't help, so it can't degrade

An unsafe tool never ships. "Converged" is a claim until a human confirms it.

The next leverage in AI isn't the prompt. It's the loop.
