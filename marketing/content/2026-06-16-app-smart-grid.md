The grid doesn't get a second chance at a bad forecast. So why do we ship load-forecasting tools that are generated once and frozen?

Picture a loop instead: a forecasting/load-balancing CLI that an **independent referee** grades against real telemetry, refactors where the error is worst, re-grades, and keeps tightening — stopping only at a target accuracy, a plateau, a budget, or a safety block. Every improvement becomes a regression test, so the tool gets *harder to break* the longer it runs.

**Smart grid** is one of 10+ applications on the **loop-engineering-anything** roadmap — alongside supply chain, quant, biotech, and more. The loop engine is built; the domain loops are open for builders.

Infrastructure is where "improve until reality says it's good" earns its keep.

Know grid/energy data? Come wire the loop. One PR adds the recipe — let's make the verified version together.
